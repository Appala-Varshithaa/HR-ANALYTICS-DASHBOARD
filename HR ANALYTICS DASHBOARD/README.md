# 📊 HR Analytics Dashboard | Power BI

## 📌 Project Overview

This project presents an interactive HR Analytics Dashboard built using Power BI to analyze employee attrition, workforce demographics, job satisfaction, and salary distribution. The dashboard enables HR professionals to identify key factors contributing to employee turnover and make data-driven workforce decisions.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Analyze overall employee attrition.
- Monitor attrition across departments, age groups, and salary slabs.
- Understand employee satisfaction by job role.
- Identify workforce trends to support HR decision-making.

## 📈 Key Insights

### Employee Attrition

- Overall attrition rate is **16.17%**.
- Most employee exits occur in the **Research & Development** department.
- Human Resources has the lowest attrition.

### Age Analysis

- Employees aged **26–35** have the highest attrition.
- Attrition decreases with increasing age.

### Salary Analysis

- Employees earning **up to 5K** show the highest attrition.
- Attrition reduces significantly as salary increases.

### Job Satisfaction

- Certain job roles such as Laboratory Technicians and Sales Executives experience higher attrition despite varying satisfaction levels.

### Experience Analysis

- Most employees leave within their first few years at the company.
- Long-tenured employees have relatively lower attrition.

---

## 💼 Business Recommendations

- Improve employee engagement for employees aged 26–35.
- Review compensation for lower salary groups.
- Focus retention strategies in the Research & Development department.
- Strengthen onboarding and career development programs during the initial years of employment.
- Monitor job satisfaction regularly to reduce voluntary attrition.
